AI Taxonomy High Level View ()
The AI Taxonomy High Level View picture the entire set of the elements of the AI Taxonomy. The AI Taxonomy provides a structured categorization of all AI concepts, techniques, methods, and domains. It aims to classify the foundational aspects of AI, including different types of AI systems, methodologies, algorithms, and core capabilities. The AI Taxonomy is a classification of Artificial Intelligence (AI) systems. It helps understand what an AI system is, what it is doing, how it works. how can be used and how it relates to other systems. The AI taxonomy is useful for the development and procurement of AI systems, as well as their deployment and monitoring. It helps understand what level of AI governance should be applied, including data management, risk management and security and ethical concerns. The AI systems are classified using different criteria: 1. based on the services they provide 2. based on how they function/ how they learn and reason 3. based on where and how they are deployed 4. based on their level of autonomy and risk The AI Taxonomy is primarily developed to organize and understand AI technologies, techniques, and capabilities and to guide development, procurement and deployment of AI systems. Note that unlike biological taxonomy, the AI taxonomy is more dynamic, and should be adapted more often to capture the new discoveries in AI technology. AI systems are different from traditional software systems and classic programming approaches. Traditional software systems are based on rules/algorithms defined solely by a human, while AI systems can derive models or algorithms from inputs or data.  While most AI systems are safe and beneficial, it's crucial to remember that they can adapt after their deployment. This self-learning capability can lead to a shift in their objectives, potentially straying from the intended purpose. It's important to be cautious and aware of these risks.   (Based on: https://yoshuabengio.org/2023/06/24/faq-on-catastrophic-ai-risks ) AI systems can be stand-alone or embedded in a product. Definition of AI System: AI system is a machine-based system that is designed to operate with varying levels of autonomy and that may exhibit adaptiveness after deployment, and that, for explicit or implicit objectives, infers, from the input it receives, how to generate outputs such as predictions, content, recommendations, or decisions that can influence physical or virtual environments; Source: https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/article/3/
